Changing Lives. Transforming Minds.

College in prisons is not ordinarily embraced. Questions and criticism are plentiful, and are mostly rooted in the stigma we often attatch to others with criminal records. The big question, is access to free education something that prisoners deserve?

According to the National Conference of State Legislatures, 1 in 3 Americans has a criminal record. That's approximately 77 million people!

Education = Cost Effective

Being in prison naturally causes lowered self-esteem and when people stop believing in themselves, they make little effort for any self-improvement. Education of any kind helps build confidence along with self-improvement. Once that feeling of success is felt by the inmate they will be more apt to want to continue to work towards their education and rehabilitation.

Education = Increased Employment Opportunities

Formerly incarcerated individuals who participated in prison education programs have access to increased and improved employment opportunities. This makes their reentry smoother and more successful, particularly since successful reentry often depends on gainful employment.

More Info

Education = Reduces Recidivism

Re-arrests are all to common, a study in 2018 by the U.S. Department of Justice found that about 68% of inmates released from state prisons were rearrested within 3 years! Education can reduce recidivism. A study done by Journal of Experimental Criminology in 2018 found that inamtes who enroll in education programs are 48% less likely to be reincarcerated than their peers who do not.
